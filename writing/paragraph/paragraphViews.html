Views and Attitude Paragraph Writing

Expressing Perspectives in a Single Paragraph for SEE Examination
Paragraph Writing Task

What is a View?

Your personal perspective or opinion on a topic. It should be clear, logical, and supported by reasoning.

What is Attitude?

The emotional tone or stance you take. It should be appropriate, consistent, and reflected through word choice.

Single Paragraph

All views and attitudes must be expressed within one coherent paragraph of approximately 150 words.

Paragraph Structure Guide

A well-structured paragraph follows this logical flow:

Topic Sentence

Clearly state your main view or attitude about the topic.

Supporting Points

Provide 2-3 reasons or arguments supporting your view.

Examples/Evidence

Include specific examples or experiences to strengthen your points.

Concluding Sentence

Reinforce your view and leave a strong final impression.

Impact of Technology on Students
In my view, technology has become a double-edged sword for students, offering incredible opportunities while presenting significant challenges that require careful navigation. Personally, I hold a cautiously optimistic attitude toward technological integration in education. On one hand, I genuinely appreciate how digital tools have democratized access to information, allowing students from all backgrounds to learn from world-class resources and connect with global perspectives. The interactive learning platforms and educational apps have made complex concepts more accessible and engaging, transforming traditional classrooms into dynamic learning environments. However, I am deeply concerned about the potential negative impacts, particularly the distraction caused by social media and gaming platforms, which can severely fragment concentration and reduce academic productivity. Furthermore, the sedentary lifestyle promoted by excessive screen time worries me, as physical activity and face-to-face social interactions are equally crucial for holistic development. Therefore, while I enthusiastically embrace technology's educational benefits, I firmly believe in establishing healthy boundaries and promoting digital literacy to ensure that technology serves as an empowering tool rather than a dominating force in students' lives.

Examiner's Marking Criteria

Your paragraph will be evaluated based on these key criteria:

Content and Ideas

Clarity of view, relevance to topic, depth of thought

• Clear thesis statement
• Relevant supporting points
• Logical reasoning

Expression and Style

Language use, vocabulary, sentence structure

• Appropriate tone
• Varied vocabulary
• Cohesive devices

Organization

Paragraph structure, logical flow, coherence

• Clear topic sentence
• Smooth transitions
• Strong conclusion

Accuracy

Grammar, spelling, punctuation, word count

• Correct grammar
• Proper punctuation
• 150±10 words

Essential Do's

  • Start with a clear topic sentence stating your view
  • Use transition words (however, therefore, furthermore)
  • Support views with specific examples or experiences
  • Maintain consistent tone throughout the paragraph
  • Use varied sentence structures for rhythm
  • Conclude by reinforcing your main view
  • Stay within 140-160 word range
  • Proofread for grammar and spelling errors

Critical Don'ts

  • Don't write multiple paragraphs
  • Avoid contradictory statements within the paragraph
  • Don't use overly emotional or biased language
  • Avoid vague statements without explanation
  • Don't exceed the word limit significantly
  • Avoid informal slang or contractions
  • Don't introduce new ideas in conclusion
  • Avoid repetitive words or phrases
