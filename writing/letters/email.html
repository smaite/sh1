Email Format Guide

  1. 1
    Sender's Email: Your professional email address
  2. 2
    Recipient's Email: Correct email of the receiver
  3. 3
    Subject Line: Clear and concise (5-8 words)
  4. 4
    Salutation: Formal greeting (Dear Sir/Madam, etc.)
  5. 5
    Introduction: State purpose clearly
  6. 6
    Body: Detailed message in paragraphs
  7. 7
    Closing: Polite ending with your name
  8. 8
    Signature: Name, designation, contact

Quick Tips

  • ✓ Keep subject line clear and specific
  • ✓ Use formal language for professional emails
  • ✓ Proofread for spelling and grammar errors
  • ✓ Be concise but complete in your message
  • ✓ Include a clear call to action if needed
  • ✓ Use paragraphs for better readability

From: anita.sharma@email.com
To: math.teacher@school.edu.np
Date: October 16, 2024
Request for Additional Mathematics Practice Problems
Dear Sir/Madam, I hope this email finds you well. I am writing to kindly request additional practice problems for our upcoming Mathematics examination, particularly focusing on Algebra and Geometry chapters. As we prepare for the SEE examination, I believe solving more varied problems would significantly enhance my understanding and problem-solving speed. While I have been practicing from our textbook, I would greatly benefit from additional challenging problems similar to those expected in the board exams. If possible, could you please share some practice sets or recommend reliable resources where I can find quality problems with solutions? I am particularly struggling with quadratic equations and circle theorems, so focused materials on these topics would be especially helpful. I understand you have a busy schedule, so I appreciate any time you can spare to guide me. I am committed to improving my performance and believe your assistance would make a substantial difference. Thank you for your continuous support and excellent teaching throughout the year. Best regards, Anita Sharma Grade 10, Section A Roll No: 15 Green Valley Secondary School

Professional Email Do's

  • ✓ Use a clear, descriptive subject line
  • ✓ Start with a formal greeting
  • ✓ State your purpose early in the email
  • ✓ Use proper paragraphs and spacing
  • ✓ Proofread before sending
  • ✓ Include a professional signature
  • ✓ Respond within 24-48 hours
  • ✓ Use appropriate font and formatting

Professional Email Don'ts

  • ✗ Don't use informal language or slang
  • ✗ Avoid vague subject lines
  • ✗ Don't write overly long paragraphs
  • ✗ Avoid excessive exclamation marks
  • ✗ Don't forget to attach mentioned files
  • ✗ Avoid using all caps (it looks like shouting)
  • ✗ Don't send without checking recipient address
  • ✗ Avoid emotional or angry language
