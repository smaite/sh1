My Dearest Friend,
I hope this letter finds you in the best of health and spirits. It feels like ages since we last met, though it has only been a few months. How quickly time flies when we're immersed in our studies and responsibilities! I've been thinking about our wonderful school days together - the laughter we shared, the secrets we exchanged, and the dreams we nurtured. Remember how we used to sit under the old banyan tree during lunch breaks, sharing our tiffin and planning our futures? Those memories remain etched in my heart like precious jewels. Life here in the city is busy but fulfilling. My SEE preparation is going well, though I must admit the pressure is sometimes overwhelming. Mathematics continues to challenge me, but I've found a wonderful tutor who makes complex problems seem simple. How are your studies progressing? I imagine you must be working hard as well. I miss our evening walks and heartfelt conversations. Letters like these help bridge the distance, but they're no substitute for your cheerful presence. Please write back soon and tell me everything about your life. I eagerly await your response. With warmest regards and fond memories,
Yours affectionately,
Anita

Personal Letter Format

  1. 1
    Sender's Address: Your address (top right)
  2. 2
    Date: Below the address
  3. 3
    Salutation: Dear [Name], or My Dear [Relation]
  4. 4
    Opening: Express affection and ask about well-being
  5. 5
    Body: Share news, feelings, and ask questions
  6. 6
    Closing: Warm wishes and expressions of affection
  7. 7
    Signature: Yours lovingly/affectionately, [Your Name]
  8. 8
    Envelope: Proper addressing with stamp
Remember: Personal letters are about sharing emotions, not just information. Write from the heart!

Expressing Emotions

  • Use affectionate language appropriate to your relationship
  • Share personal feelings and experiences honestly
  • Include specific memories you cherish together
  • Express concern for their well-being
  • Use descriptive language to paint vivid pictures
  • Balance sharing about yourself with asking about them
  • End with warm wishes and anticipation for their reply

Writing Techniques

  • Start with a warm, personal greeting
  • Maintain a conversational, friendly tone
  • Use paragraphs to organize different thoughts
  • Include questions to encourage response
  • Share news in chronological or importance order
  • Use anecdotes and personal stories
  • Keep between 150-200 words for SEE exams
  • Proofread for spelling and clarity
