Recipe Writing as a Craft

At an advanced level, recipe writing is not merely about listing steps. It is a disciplined form of instructional writing that balances accuracy, sequencing, tone, and reader psychology.

A well-written recipe anticipates the reader’s questions, eliminates ambiguity, and maintains logical flow. Every verb, quantity, and transition matters. The writer must sound confident, neutral, and precise, guiding the reader from preparation to presentation without confusion.

Clarity

Instructions must be unambiguous and measurable.

Sequence

Each step follows a natural and practical order.

Economy of Language

No unnecessary words, no missing logic.

Consistency

Uniform tense, tone, and measurement style.

Advanced examiners look for control, not decoration. A recipe impresses through precision, not poetry.

Model Recipes (Advanced Standard)

Grilled Cheese Sandwich

Ingredients: Two slices of bread, two slices of cheese, one tablespoon of butter, and a pinch of black pepper.

Method:
  1. First, butter one side of each bread slice evenly.
  2. Next, place a cheese slice between the unbuttered sides and sprinkle black pepper lightly.
  3. Then, heat a pan or grill over medium heat.
  4. After that, grill the sandwich until both sides turn golden brown.
  5. Finally, remove carefully and serve hot once the cheese has melted.

Vegetable Soup

Ingredients: One cup of chopped mixed vegetables, one tablespoon of butter, one teaspoon of garlic paste, three cups of water, salt and pepper to taste.

Method:
  1. First, heat butter in a saucepan and sauté the garlic paste lightly.
  2. Next, add the chopped vegetables and cook for two minutes.
  3. Then, pour in water and bring the mixture to a boil.
  4. After that, reduce heat and allow it to simmer until the vegetables are soft.
  5. Finally, season with salt and pepper and serve hot.

Simple Pancakes

Ingredients: One cup of flour, one egg, one cup of milk, one teaspoon of baking powder, two tablespoons of sugar, and oil for frying.

Method:
  1. First, mix flour, sugar, and baking powder in a bowl.
  2. Next, add the egg and milk gradually, stirring to form a smooth batter.
  3. Then, heat a lightly oiled pan over medium heat.
  4. After that, pour a ladle of batter onto the pan and cook until bubbles appear.
  5. Finally, flip the pancake, cook briefly, and serve warm.

Boiled Eggs

Ingredients: Two eggs, enough water to cover them, and a pinch of salt.

Method:
  1. First, place the eggs gently in a saucepan.
  2. Next, add enough water to cover the eggs completely.
  3. Then, bring the water to a boil over medium heat.
  4. After that, continue boiling for seven to eight minutes.
  5. Finally, remove the eggs, cool them, and peel before serving.

Practice Topics for Notebook Writing

Write any one of the following recipes in your notebook. Your recipe should be between 120–150 words and must follow the same professional format and cohesive sequencing used in the model recipes above.

  • Chicken Momo
  • Vegetable Chowmein
  • Sel Roti
  • Chocolate Cake
  • Paneer Curry
Before submitting, check whether your method uses clear sequencing words and whether each step logically follows the previous one.

Examiner’s Lens

  • Logical sequencing of steps
  • Use of imperative verbs
  • Consistency of tense
  • Accuracy of measurements
  • Formal and neutral tone
  • Neat paragraphing
