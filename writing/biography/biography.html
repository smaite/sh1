Biography Writing

Present a life story clearly, factually, and effectively

How to Write a Biography (Step-by-Step)

  1. First, write the full name of the person and basic introduction.
  2. Next, mention birth details and family background.
  3. Then, describe education and early life experiences.
  4. After that, highlight major achievements and contributions.
  5. Now, include awards, qualities, or impact on society.
  6. Finally, conclude with the person’s importance or inspiration.
Exam Tip: Write in third person, use past tense (if the person is no longer alive), and keep the length around 130 words.

Sample Biographies (Click to Reveal)

Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi was a great leader and the father of the Indian nation. He was born on 2nd October 1869 in Porbandar, India. He studied law in England and later worked in South Africa, where he began his fight against injustice. Gandhi believed in truth, non-violence, and peaceful protest. He led India’s freedom movement through non-violent methods and inspired millions of people. His simple lifestyle, strong moral values, and leadership made him respected all over the world. He was assassinated in 1948, but his ideals continue to guide people even today.

Helen Keller

Helen Keller was a remarkable American author and social activist. She was born in 1880 and lost her sight and hearing at an early age due to illness. With the help of her teacher, Anne Sullivan, she learned to communicate and receive education. Helen Keller later graduated from college and wrote several books. She worked tirelessly for the rights of disabled people and inspired the world through her courage and determination. Her life proves that physical disabilities cannot stop a person from achieving greatness.

Bhanubhakta Acharya

Bhanubhakta Acharya was a famous Nepali poet known as the Adikavi of Nepal. He was born in 1814 in Tanahun district. He is best remembered for translating the Ramayana into simple Nepali language, making it accessible to common people. His literary works played a vital role in the development of Nepali literature. Bhanubhakta’s dedication, creativity, and love for language made him a respected figure in Nepali society. His contributions are still remembered and celebrated today.
