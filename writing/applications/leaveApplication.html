Instructions for Writing Leave Applications

  • Be concise and to the point - Remember the 150-word limit.
  • Use proper format - Include sender's address, date, salutation, subject, body, closing, and signature.
  • State the purpose clearly - Mention the type of leave and duration upfront.
  • Provide a valid reason - Be honest and specific about why you need leave.
  • Mention arrangements made - If applicable, state how your responsibilities will be handled.
  • Use polite and formal language - Maintain a respectful tone throughout.
  • Proofread carefully - Check for spelling, grammar, and punctuation errors.
Important: A leave application is a formal document. Always address the recipient properly (e.g., "Respected Sir/Madam") and end with a polite closing (e.g., "Yours faithfully").

Structure of a Leave Application

1. Sender's Address (Top left)
2. Date (Below address)
3. Recipient's Address
4. Salutation (Respected Sir/Madam)
5. Subject Line (Clear and concise)
6. Body Paragraphs:
  - Introduction & purpose
  - Reason for leave
  - Duration of leave
  - Arrangements made (if any)
7. Closing (Yours faithfully/obediently)
8. Signature
9. Your Name

Key Phrases to Use

  • "I am writing to request leave for..."
  • "I would be grateful if you could grant me leave..."
  • "I assure you that I will complete my pending work..."
  • "Thank you for considering my request."
