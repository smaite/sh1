Paragraph Structure

What is a Paragraph?

A paragraph is a group of related sentences that discuss one main idea. It consists of a topic sentence, supporting sentences, and a concluding sentence.

Components of a Paragraph

  • Topic Sentence: States the main idea (usually first sentence)
  • Supporting Sentences: Explain, describe, or prove the main idea
  • Concluding Sentence: Summarizes or restates the main idea

Qualities of a Good Paragraph

  • Unity: All sentences support one main idea
  • Coherence: Ideas flow logically with transition words
  • Completeness: Paragraph is fully developed
  • Length: Usually 5-8 sentences for SEE level

Transition Words

  • Addition: moreover, furthermore, in addition, also
  • Contrast: however, on the other hand, nevertheless
  • Examples: for example, for instance, such as
  • Conclusion: in conclusion, therefore, thus, finally

Writing Tips

  • Start with a clear topic sentence
  • Use specific examples and details
  • Check for grammar and spelling
  • Read aloud to check flow
