Writing Instructions

What are Instructions?

Instructions are clear, step-by-step directions that tell someone how to do something or how to use something.

Key Features

  • Title: Clear indication of what the instructions are for
  • List of Materials: What is needed (if applicable)
  • Numbered Steps: Logical sequence of actions
  • Imperative Verbs: Command forms (Cut, Mix, Open, etc.)
  • Simple Language: Clear and easy to understand

Common Imperative Verbs

  • Preparation: Take, Get, Collect, Gather, Prepare
  • Action: Cut, Mix, Add, Pour, Stir, Open, Close
  • Sequence: First, Then, Next, After that, Finally
  • Warning: Be careful, Make sure, Ensure, Do not

Structure

  1. Title: How to [Action]
  2. Introduction (optional): Brief explanation of purpose
  3. Materials Needed: List of items required
  4. Step-by-Step Instructions: Numbered or bulleted list
  5. Conclusion/Tips: Additional advice or warnings

Writing Tips

  • Start each step with an action verb
  • Keep steps short and simple
  • Use sequence markers (First, Next, Then, Finally)
  • Include warnings for dangerous steps
  • Test your instructions - would a beginner understand?
