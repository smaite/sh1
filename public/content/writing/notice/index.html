Notice Writing

Write clear and concise notices

Notice Format

What is a Notice?

A notice is a formal written or printed announcement displayed publicly to inform people about events, meetings, competitions, or important information.

Format Components

  • Name of Institution/Organization: At the top (centered)
  • Heading: "NOTICE" in capital letters
  • Date: When the notice is issued
  • Title/Subject: Brief description of the notice
  • Body: Details of the event/information
  • Signature: Name and designation of the issuer

Types of Notices

  • Event Notice: Sports day, cultural program, competition
  • Meeting Notice: PTA meeting, staff meeting
  • Warning Notice: Rules violation, discipline
  • Information Notice: Holidays, exam schedule, results
  • Auction/Sale Notice: Lost and found, book sale

What to Include

  • What: Name and nature of the event
  • When: Date and time
  • Where: Venue/location
  • Who: Target audience/participants
  • How: Registration process or entry details

Writing Tips

  • Use formal, simple language
  • Keep it brief (50-80 words for SEE)
  • Put important information first
  • Use clear headings and bullet points if needed
  • Don't use first person (I, we)
  • Include complete contact information

Practice Topics

Easy Level

Lost and found, Holiday notice

Medium Level

Meeting notice, Competition announcement

Hard Level

Event organization, Fundraising
