Dialogue Writing

Create authentic conversations

Dialogue Format

What is a Dialogue?

A dialogue is a written conversation between two or more people. It represents spoken language and includes the actual words exchanged between speakers.

Types of Dialogues

  • Formal Dialogue: Between officials, teachers, strangers
  • Informal Dialogue: Between friends, family members
  • Professional Dialogue: Job interviews, business talks
  • Situational Dialogue: At a shop, hospital, bank

Format Rules

  • Speaker Name: Write at the beginning of each line
  • Colon: Use after the speaker's name
  • Quotation Marks: Enclose spoken words
  • New Line: Each speaker gets a new line
  • Stage Directions: Actions in brackets (optional)

Sample Format

Ram: "Good morning, Sita. How are you?"

Sita: "Good morning, Ram. I'm fine, thank you. How about you?"

Ram: "I'm doing well. Are you ready for the exam?"

Sita: "Yes, I've prepared well. I hope to get good marks."

Ram: "Best of luck! See you at school."

Sita: "Thank you. Goodbye!"

Dialogue Language

  • Greetings: Hello, Hi, Good morning, Namaste
  • Opening: How are you? How do you do?
  • Response: I'm fine, Not bad, Pretty good
  • Closing: Goodbye, See you, Take care, Bye

Writing Tips

  • Make it natural and realistic
  • Match language to the characters (formal/informal)
  • Keep sentences short in spoken dialogue
  • Include appropriate greetings and closings
  • Stay on topic throughout the conversation
  • Each speaker should contribute equally

Common Situations

  • At a doctor's clinic
  • In a classroom
  • At a restaurant/shop
  • Planning a trip/event
  • Discussing studies/career
  • Asking for/giving directions
