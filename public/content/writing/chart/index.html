Chart Interpretation

Learn to analyze and describe data from charts and graphs

Understanding Charts

Types of Charts

  • Bar Charts: Compare quantities across categories
  • Pie Charts: Show proportions/percentages of a whole
  • Line Graphs: Show trends over time
  • Tables: Organize data in rows and columns

Steps to Interpret

  1. Read the Title: Understand what the chart shows
  2. Check Labels: Look at axes, legends, and units
  3. Identify Key Data: Find highest, lowest, and trends
  4. Compare Data: Notice differences and similarities
  5. Draw Conclusions: Summarize the main findings

Writing Structure

  • Introduction: State what the chart shows
  • Body: Describe the data with specific numbers
  • Conclusion: Summarize main findings

Useful Vocabulary

  • Increase: rise, grow, climb, soar, jump
  • Decrease: fall, drop, decline, plunge, plummet
  • Stable: remain constant, stay the same, level off
  • Comparison: whereas, while, compared to, in contrast

Sample Sentence Starters

  • "The chart shows/displayed/illustrates..."
  • "According to the graph,..."
  • "The highest/lowest figure was..."
  • "There was a significant increase/decrease in..."
  • "In conclusion, the data indicates that..."
