News Article Structure

What is News Writing?

News writing is the style of writing used to report events in a factual, objective, and concise manner. It follows the inverted pyramid structure, putting the most important information first.

Inverted Pyramid Structure

  • Lead/Headline: Most important information (who, what, when, where, why)
  • Body: Supporting details, quotes, background
  • Conclusion: Least important information

Components

  • Headline: Catchy, summarizes the main point
  • Byline: Name of the reporter
  • Dateline: Place and date of the event
  • Lead Paragraph: Answers 5 W's and 1 H
  • Body: Details in order of importance
  • Conclusion: Additional information or future implications

The 5 W's and 1 H

  • Who: Who is involved?
  • What: What happened?
  • When: When did it happen?
  • Where: Where did it happen?
  • Why: Why did it happen?
  • How: How did it happen?

News Language

  • Active Voice: "Police arrested the thief" not "The thief was arrested"
  • Present Tense: For recent events
  • Short Sentences: Easy to read and understand
  • No Opinion: Objective reporting only

Writing Tips

  • Start with the most newsworthy fact
  • Be factual and objective - no bias
  • Use direct quotes when possible
  • Verify facts before publishing
  • Keep paragraphs short (2-3 sentences)
  • Include specific details (names, dates, numbers)
