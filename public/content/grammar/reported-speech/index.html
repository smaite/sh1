Reported Speech

Convert direct to indirect speech

Reporting Rules

Direct Speech vs Indirect Speech

Direct: He said, "I am tired."

Indirect: He said that he was tired.

Key Changes

  • Remove quotation marks and use 'that'
  • Change pronouns: I → he/she, my → his/her, etc.
  • Backshift tenses: Present → Past
  • Time expressions: now → then, today → that day, tomorrow → the next day

Tense Backshifting

  • Simple Present → Simple Past (says → said)
  • Present Continuous → Past Continuous (is playing → was playing)
  • Present Perfect → Past Perfect (has done → had done)
  • Simple Past → Past Perfect (did → had done)
  • Will → Would, Can → Could, May → Might

Reporting Verbs

  • Statements: said, told, stated, mentioned
  • Questions: asked, inquired, wondered
  • Commands/Requests: ordered, requested, advised, suggested
