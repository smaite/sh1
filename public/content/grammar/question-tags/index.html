Question Tag Rules

What are Question Tags?

Short questions added at the end of statements to confirm information or invite agreement.

Example: You are coming, aren't you?

The Golden Rule

  • Positive statement → Negative tag
  • Negative statement → Positive tag

Formation Rules

  • Use the same auxiliary verb as in the main sentence
  • Use the same subject pronoun (I, you, he, she, it, we, they)
  • Use contracted negative forms (isn't, aren't, wasn't, weren't, don't, doesn't, didn't, haven't, hasn't, hadn't, won't, wouldn't, can't, couldn't)

Examples

  • She is a doctor, isn't she?
  • They don't like coffee, do they?
  • He has finished his work, hasn't he?
  • You will help me, won't you?
  • Let's go for a walk, shall we?
  • I'm right, aren't I?

Special Cases

  • Nobody/No one/Nothing → positive tag (Nobody came, did they?)
  • Let's → shall we
  • Imperatives → will you/won't you
