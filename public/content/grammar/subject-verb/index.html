Subject-Verb Agreement

Grammar agreement rules

Agreement Rules

Basic Rule

A singular subject takes a singular verb, and a plural subject takes a plural verb.

  • Singular: The boy runs fast.
  • Plural: The boys run fast.

Key Rules

1. Singular subjects with 's/es' verbs
  • He/She/It + verb+s/es (He walks, She sings, It works)
  • Singular nouns: The cat sleeps, The book is
2. Plural subjects with base verbs
  • They/We/You + base verb (They play, We study)
  • Plural nouns: The cats sleep, The books are
3. Special Cases
  • Neither/Either: Singular (Neither of them is here)
  • Each/Every: Singular (Each student has a book)
  • Anyone/Someone: Singular (Someone is knocking)
  • Many/Most: Plural (Many students are absent)
4. Collective Nouns
  • Team, family, group, committee - can be singular or plural depending on context
  • The team is winning (as a unit)
  • The team are arguing (as individuals)
5. Subject Separated from Verb
  • Ignore words between subject and verb
  • The boy, along with his friends, is coming.
  • One of the students has passed.
6. 'There is/are' and 'Here is/are'
  • Look at the noun after the verb
  • There is a book on the table.
  • There are books on the table.
